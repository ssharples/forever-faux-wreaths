# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary users are UK shoppers looking for handcrafted faux wreaths for seasonal decor, memorial moments, gifts, and bespoke floral pieces. They arrive in a browsing mindset, often on mobile, and need to understand product style, size, availability, price, delivery expectations, and how to commission something custom.

The secondary users are the Forever Faux Wreaths team using `/admin` to manage products, orders, enquiries, gallery images, reviews, customers, and site settings. In admin areas the register shifts to product UI: clear task flow, dense but readable information, predictable controls, and reliable empty, loading, and error states.

## Product Purpose

Forever Faux Wreaths sells ready-made and bespoke faux floral wreaths from Preston, Lancashire. The site should make the craft and product photography feel trustworthy, help customers move from browsing to cart or enquiry, and give the team a practical control room for inventory and content.

Success means visitors can quickly judge the quality and suitability of a wreath, complete checkout through Stripe with confidence, or submit a bespoke enquiry without friction. For admin users, success means content and order operations are obvious, resilient, and fast enough for regular use.

## Brand Personality

Warm, handcrafted, and considered. The storefront should feel personal and floral without becoming twee, luxury-coded without feeling cold, and clear enough that ecommerce tasks remain easy.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards on customer-facing pages, template card grids that make the products feel interchangeable, low-contrast pale text used for elegance, decorative glassmorphism, excessive rounded cards, and copy that sounds broad rather than specific to wreaths, flowers, seasons, memorial pieces, delivery, and bespoke making.

For admin, avoid decorative brand flourishes that slow task work, display fonts in dense controls, inconsistent button or table treatment, and hidden workflow states.

## Design Principles

Show the wreath before explaining it. Product imagery and concrete details should carry the first impression on customer pages.

Keep the handmade signal precise. Use warmth through materials, photography, type, and specific copy, not vague sentiment or decorative clutter.

Make commerce feel calm. Cart, checkout, product filters, forms, and delivery information should be direct, legible, and confidence-building.

Separate brand mood from admin utility. The admin area can borrow the palette, but it should prioritise scannable layouts, consistent controls, and task completion.

Protect mobile shoppers. Every customer route should work cleanly on small screens with readable type, stable image aspect ratios, and touch targets at least 44px.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve keyboard access, visible focus indicators, semantic headings and landmarks, descriptive link and button text, useful image alt text, form labels and errors, reduced-motion alternatives, and contrast of at least 4.5:1 for body text and form helper text.
